Title: Complex Series and Euler Formula Summary

Complex Taylor series extend Taylor series to complex variables.

For complex z:

e^z = 1 + z + z^2/2! + z^3/3! + ...

If z = iθ:

e^(iθ) = 1 + iθ + (iθ)^2/2! + (iθ)^3/3! + ...

Powers of i:

i^0 = 1
i^1 = i
i^2 = -1
i^3 = -i
i^4 = 1

Separating real and imaginary parts gives Euler's formula:

e^(iθ) = cos(θ) + i sin(θ)

Euler's formula is used in waves, oscillations, Fourier analysis, quantum mechanics, electromagnetism, and signal processing.
