Title: Physics Applications of Taylor Series and Complex Exponentials

Taylor series applications in physics:

1. Small-angle approximation:
sin(θ) ≈ θ for small θ.
This is used in pendulum motion.

2. Approximation near equilibrium:
Many physical systems are approximated near stable equilibrium points.

3. Quantum mechanics:
Perturbation theory uses series approximations.

4. Relativity:
When v is much smaller than c, relativistic expressions can be approximated using Taylor series.

5. Optics:
Small-angle approximations are used in paraxial optics.

Complex exponential applications:

1. Oscillations
2. Waves
3. Fourier analysis
4. Quantum wavefunctions
5. Electromagnetic waves

Euler's formula allows sine and cosine waves to be written as complex exponentials.
This makes differential equations easier to solve.
